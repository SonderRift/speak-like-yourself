Sound familiar?

Why you still freeze in Portuguese

  • Real conversation starts and your mind goes blank.
  • You translate every sentence in your head first.
  • You sound stiff, robotic, straight out of a textbook.
  • Brazilians speak fast and everything blurs together.
  • You never know what is natural vs technically correct.

None of this means you are bad at languages. It means nobody taught you how to stay in a conversation. That is a skill, and it can be learned first, not last.

The method

Three layers, in the order
that actually works

1

Survival

Rescue phrases that keep you in the conversation when you get stuck: slow people down, ask for words, buy time, stay calm. You learn to swim before the deep end.

You can already sayPode falar mais devagar, por favor?
2

Self-Expression

Anchored on "Eu": you build natural sentences about your own life with real vocabulary, slang, and expressions in context. Not drills. Your words, your world.

You can already sayEu trabalho com design. E você?
3

Fluency

Expand to any topic, any person, natural speed. The effort shifts from thinking to flowing. This is where Portuguese stops being a subject and becomes yours.

You can already sayA gente se vê sábado, então. Fechou!

Questions

Quick answers

I am a complete beginner. Is this for me?

Yes. Complete beginners are my specialty. The survival layer exists exactly so you can function in Portuguese long before you feel "ready".

Do you teach grammar?

Yes, but in context and on demand, when it answers a question you actually have. Grammar serves the conversation, not the other way around.

What happens in a trial lesson?

It is a test drive plus roadmap, not a sample class. You will speak Portuguese in the first minutes, I will spot your patterns, and you leave knowing exactly what your path looks like.
